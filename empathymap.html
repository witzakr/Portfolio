The empathy map was part of my User-Centered Design (UCD) assignment, and it's considered one of the initial steps in design thinking. This map is like a tool for teamwork that helps us really get what a specific type of user is all about. It's a way for us to put down what we already know about users so that everyone on the team is on the same page about what users really need. Plus, it makes it easier for us to make decisions together. I used a template for this assignment, and you can find it on the Miro website. The topic of the empathy map was " I am tired at school" and I divided it into "thinks", "does", "says" and "feels". I also included "pains" and "gains". It was a cool experience working on this task, and I believe it's going to be handy in the future as I continue to explore User-Centered Des. https://miro.com/templates/empathy-map/
