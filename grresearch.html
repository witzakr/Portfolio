For the research for our group project, we conducted a survey and interview and we created multiple personas and a competitor analysis. The point of each is:
- Interview: The interview is targeted to people with web development experience and the way questions are formulated is to get advice and professional expertise on a topic that will be crucial in the later development of the project.
- Survey: The survey is targeted to people who are potential users of the portfolio website. The answers from this survey will be crucial because after we get them, we will group them up, and create a graph, and based on the information displayed there, we will build our website.
- Personas: Personas are fictional characters, that you create based on your research to represent the different user types that might use your service, product, site, or brand in a similar way. We will create those personas based on the survey answers.
- Competitor analysis: Competitor analysis provides strategic insights into the features, functions, flows, and feelings evoked by the design solutions of your competitors. By understanding these facets of competitors’ products, you can strategically design your solution with the goal of making a superior product and/or experience.
- Card sorting: a method used to help design or evaluate the information architecture of a site. In a card sorting session, participants organize topics into categories that make sense to them and they may also help you label these groups.
